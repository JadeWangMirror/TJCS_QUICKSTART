# Objective
The final project is a cornerstone of this course, designed to help you synthesize the concepts we've covered—from rasterization and PBR to ray tracing and neural rendering. Working in small groups, you will delve deep into a chosen area, producing a significant piece of work that demonstrates both technical understanding and creative or critical thinking.

---

## Track 1: Advanced Visual Effects
**Focus:** Pushing the boundaries of image quality and physical simulation using advanced rendering techniques.

**Objective:**  
Create a static or pre-rendered scene that showcases complex photorealistic lighting, advanced material properties, or a novel physical effect. The primary goal is to achieve a high-fidelity visual outcome.

**Technical Core:**  
- Path Tracing, Monte Carlo Ray Tracing, or advanced Physically Based Rendering (PBR).

**Potential Topics:**  
- **Complex Global Illumination:** Multiple-bounce diffuse lighting in a complex environment.  
- **Advanced Materials:** Subsurface scattering (e.g., wax, skin), volumetric materials (smoke, fog), custom anisotropic BRDFs (brushed metal, cloth).  
- **Specialized Effects:** Physically accurate depth-of-field, motion blur, or a simulation of a specific phenomenon like diffraction or iridescence.

---

## Track 2: Real-Time Interactive Application
**Focus:** Building an interactive application that leverages modern real-time rendering techniques.

**Objective:**  
Develop a small game, an interactive demo, or a real-time visualization tool that highlights real-time graphics capabilities. The key challenge is balancing visual fidelity with performance.

**Technical Core:**  
- OpenGL, PBR

**Potential Features:**  
- Dynamic lighting and shadows  
- Image-Based Lighting (IBL) for ambient reflection  
- Post-processing effects (e.g., HDR tone-mapping, bloom, SSAO)  
- A simple game mechanic or interactive camera that allows users to explore the scene and its materials

---

## Track 3: Paper Research & Improvement
**Focus:** Critical analysis and practical implementation of recent graphics research, with a focus on innovation.

**Objective:**  
Select a recent research paper from the provided list or within 3yrs Siggraph/CVPR conferences, implement its core contribution, rigorously analyze its strengths and weaknesses, and propose and implement a meaningful improvement.

**Process:**  
1. **Implementation & Analysis:** Faithfully reproduce the core algorithm or technique. Then, perform a critical analysis: Where does it excel? Where does it fail? What are its limitations (e.g., performance, quality, generality)?  
2. **Innovation:** Propose a concrete improvement, extension, or hybrid approach to address one of the identified weaknesses. This is the most important part of the project. Simply replicating the paper is not sufficient.
